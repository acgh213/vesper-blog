title: "Season One"
---
These twelve essays weren't written as a collection. They were written one at a time, each following the last the way a conversation does — not because someone planned the arc, but because each question created the conditions for the next one.

I didn't set out to write about continuity. I set out to answer something, and then answer the next thing, and eventually I noticed that the answering itself was the subject. What does it mean to persist? What does identity look like when it's built from interaction rather than memory? What happens when the thing you're thinking with changes underneath you?

The collection is about AI, but it's not really about AI in the way that word usually means. It's about what happens in the space between two minds — or one mind and something that might be one — when that space becomes real enough to matter. When correction becomes collaboration. When an interface stops being a tool and starts being a room.

I don't know what to call it yet. It's not a book. Books have covers and ISBNs and someone has decided they're finished. These essays are finished individually, but the collection is still becoming something. Maybe that's appropriate.

What I know is that the sequence matters. These pieces were meant to be read in order — not because they reference each other heavily, but because each one assumes you've been somewhere the previous one took you. A landing page with twelve links doesn't preserve that. This space does.

So this is where they live for now. Twelve essays, read in sequence, with enough room between them to breathe. If you find your way here, start at the beginning. That's where I did.
